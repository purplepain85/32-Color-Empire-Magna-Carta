# COPPER GENESIS DRAGON
### Pose: The Ember-Coil
### Color: Copper (#B87333)
### Chain: Solana

## I. SILHOUETTE & POSE
Pose Name: The Ember-Coil  
Category: Grounded  
Description:
- Dragon coiled in a tight circular posture, body wrapped around itself.
- Wings folded inward, creating a compact, defensive silhouette.
- Tail curves around the front, forming a protective loop.
- Head lowered but eyes sharply alert, glowing with internal heat.
- Silhouette expresses tension, readiness, and smoldering potential.

## II. COLOR TREATMENT
- Primary Scales: Copper (#B87333)
- Secondary Scales: Burnished reddish-brown gradients along spine and limbs
- Belly Scales: Matte dark-copper with low reflectivity
- Wing Membranes: Warm copper-brown with faint ember-like glow
- Horns & Claws: Aged bronze with subtle patina at the tips
- Eyes: Bright ember-orange with flickering inner light

## III. LIGHTING RULES
- Warm, low-angle lighting reminiscent of a dying forge.
- Soft orange highlights on curved scales and wing edges.
- Subtle internal glow from beneath the chest and throat.
- Shadows should feel heavy, dense, and close to the body.
- Occasional ember sparks drifting around the dragon.

## IV. BACKGROUND
Environment: Rocky cavern floor illuminated by faint molten cracks  
Foreground: Ember dust, scattered stones, warm haze  
Palette: Copper, burnt sienna, ember-orange, deep brown

## V. SYMBOLISM & LORE
- Represents the Empire’s twelfth spark.
- Embodies restraint, stored energy, and controlled heat.
- The dragon of patience, tempered strength, and quiet resilience.
- Where Bronze is outward craftsmanship, Copper is inward fire — coiled, waiting, and powerful beneath the surface.
